TL;DR*: The Short Version

Born and raised in Zimbabwe**, a true African at heart, I care deeply about people.
And so I moved to the Netherlands to study psychology.
However, when the pandemic hit, I too went through
the Covid-19 Life Reassessment¬©,
and I decided I want something a bit different.

*enter Software Engineering*

I joined the Generation Tech Talent Accelerator program, learnt a whole bunch of coding, did some really cool projects, and it changed my life.

*TL;DR: Too Long; Don't Read

**though I am also a British citizen, with working rights in the UK.

TS;WM*: The Longer Version

(Part 1)

I've always been curious about the way the world works, and not just how
it works, but why it works that way.

And so, I fell in love with software engineering when I realised how much it allows me to be myself - it is so logical, and there is an explanation for almost everything,
it fulfils my love of maths and physics, all while scratching my creative itch.

I'm also interested in how tech can help the people I see around me, and
how tech allows us to build things to solve real world problems.

*TS;WM: Too Short; Want More

I was very blessed to enrol in the Generation Tech Talent Accelerator program,
which has given me the opportunity to learn and implement in practice languages
such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript (and TypeScript for good measure!),

as well as types of programming such as functional programming, and objected-oriented programming, and source control with Git.

We learnt about how to be and think like software engineers, developing a growth mindset, cultivating perseverance, and personal responsibility.

I also met some really great humans, and got to work on some pretty great projects!

"The key to a wonderful life is to never stop wandering into wonder."
Suzy Kassem

When I'm not coding, you can find me cultivating wonder in my life through spending time in the great outdoors, playing the clarinet, drawing, or hanging out with some lovely humans.

I love to laugh, and two of my favourite words are petrichor* and psithurism**, and I've always been intrigued by the way lifts work.

'Suits' is my current binge-watch, and memes are one of my love languages.

*petrichor - the earthy scent produced when rain falls on dry soil
**psithurism - the sound of rustling leaves, or wind through the trees
